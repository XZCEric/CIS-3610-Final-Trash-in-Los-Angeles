Trash in Los Angeles

Introduction Challenges Solutions Safe Disposal

Introduction

On the streets of Los Angeles all kinds of trash like paper, furniture, food waste is being thrown on the curbside, on the road and it is everywhere. This is not only affecting the cleanness of the street but also affecting the living conditions of people living in the area. People are more likely to be exposed to toxic chemicals that came from any electronic waste or horrible smells from food waste. Now about 5,200 tons of solid waste are being produced everyday in L.A. most of them go to landfills waiting to be decomposed, but there is an uncalculated amount of trash that will stay on the curbside, middle of street waiting to be picked up, or sitting there forever.

Illegal dumping

Illegal dumping is discarding any waste, whether it is oil, furniture, appliances, trash, litter or landscaping cuttings, etc., upon any public right-of-way, City property or private property, without consent of the owner. Amount of illegal dumping in the city of L.A. has jumped 450% from 2016 to 2020. The ever increasing amount of trash on the streets and alleyways, making it unsafe for pedestrians, cyclists, drivers and all residents in impacted neighborhoods. Although if you get caught for illegal dumping you may pay a fine up to $10,000 and can end up to six months in jail, but there is no one who watches over the street and catch who is dumping this trash onto the street.

Small Trashcans

There are over 750,000 families who live in the city of Los Angeles, and a recorded 6,500 tons of trash is being collected daily by L.A. sanitation (not counting the ones dumped on the street). Trash gets collected on a scheduled collection day and gets picked up weekly. In L.A. we use these trash bins that can hold up to 50 pounds of trash. Sometimes those trash cans get filled up quickly, and people would have nowhere to put their trash, and it will be just left on the ground, causing it to stack up more and more over time and can cause blocking of way and horrible smells.

No Trash Pickups

Although L.A. sanitation does a very hard job to try to keep the city of L.A. clean, sometimes they can run into trouble trying to pick up trash. For example, like large pieces of furniture being disposed of on the street, their trash cars are not meant to pick up things like that. There could also be harmful electronic waste that needs to be handled carefully, and sometimes there is food waste scattered on the street side making it impossible to pick up. The most important thing is that while issues like this keep happening there is no one to come and clean and pick them up. So the condition just keeps getting worse and worse until someone decides that it is too much and they want to clean it up.

Solutions

Ways to produce less trash


  • This can be done easier than you think, there are 6500 Tons of trash produced every day, and almost a quarter of that is food waste. Just imagine how much food is being wasted there. What we can do instead is buy what you can eat, don’t buy an excessive amount and put it in the freezer, wait for it to go bad, and then throw it away.

  • Bring your own fabric bag to the store and use less plastic bags, plus the fabric bag usually costs less than a dollar, and sometimes there are people passing them out for free.

  • Use a reusable bottle instead of drinking from a plastic bottle.

  • Use an electric razor rather than throwaway plastic razors and blade cartridges.

  • Electronics are an important part of our life now, either laptop, phone, printer, but do you really need to buy the newest iphone everytime one comes out? Not really, right? This way there will be less harmful electronic waste produced.

  • Donate clothes or shoes that you don’t use any more. They are going to waste anyways, why don’t you help someone instead!

  • Going paperless for your bills, I’m sure a lot of companies like Chase, Bofa, Southern California Edison, etc offer paperless bills, this will reduce the amount of paper wasted.

  • Recycling is also an important part of producing less trash, we will go into more detail of this in the next section.

Ways to recycle trash


  • Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. There is a lot of stuff you can actually recycle, first let’s begin with a simple one that is a plastic bottle, utensils and containers, although it is best to try not using them, but it’s hard since a lot of drinks, food come with it. There are a lot of bottle recycling centers like rePlanet, ponce recycling center, etc., you can find one around your neighborhood. (All links of recycling center will be down below)

  • Glass and metal bottles can also be recycled in the bottle recycling center.

  • Newspaper, magazines, junk mail, printer paper, can all be recycled in a paper recycling center, or simply put in the recycle trash bins if you have one in your neighborhood.

  • Cardboard needs to be recycled separately from the other paper object, they need to be clean, wet ones, or greasy pizza boxes can’t be recycled.


  • Wood objects, like one time use chopsticks, left over wood chips from crafting somethings can also be recycled.

  • If you have a garden you can try composting things like banana peels, strawberry leafs, and other vegetable and fruit leftovers to make organic fertilizer. (ONLY PUT NON-ANIMAL SCRAPS, LEFTOVER MEAT WON'T WORK)

  • Old clothes that can’t be donated can also be recycled in a cloth recycling center, or you can cut your old clothes and turn them into rags for cleaning.

  • Electronic waste like used phones, cameras, batteries can be recycled at a proper electronic waste recycle center. (some electronic waste can’t be recycled, and need to be disposed properly, will go in more detail down below)

Safe Disposal

Safe disposal of large pieces of trash

When you switch that old sofa to a new soft comfy one, what do you do with the old one? The wrong thing to do is just putting it on the streetside and blocking the roadway. The easiest thing to do is drop it off at your local bulky item drop-off spot, or if you don’t have a large enough car you can call them at 1-800-773-2489 (only for LA county area) or use the MyLA311 app (only for LA county area) to schedule a curbside pick-up. Something else you can do is to donate it to charity or a non-profit organization, if the furniture is going to waste why don’t you use it to help other people out instead. (Things like sofa, fridge & freezer, carpet all fit into this category)

Dispose hazardous waste safely

Hazardous waste like paint, mercury light bulbs, garden chemicals, oven cleaners or batteries need to be disposed properly, or it can release toxic chemicals into the air and can cause symptoms like headache or vomiting. Please don’t dump these on the ground because there is liquid waste like paint and mercury that is very hard to clean, and this liquid waste needs to be separated from the rest. LA Sanitation (LASAN) has established programs for Los Angeles City and L.A. County residents to safely dispose their household hazardous waste. This waste can be dropped off at permanent collection sites known as S.A.F.E. Centers or mobile collection events located throughout the City of Los Angeles.

Safe disposal of sharps

Sharps/needles should not be thrown into the trash cans, because it can cause cross contamination. After the use of a sharp immediately place used needles and other sharps in a sharps disposal container to reduce the risk of needle sticks, cuts or punctures from loose sharps. DO use an FDA-cleared sharps disposal container, if possible. Then you may be able to drop off your sharps disposal containers at nearby collections sites, such as doctors' offices, hospitals, pharmacies, health departments, medical waste facilities and a police or fire stations. If you can go to the doctor’s office or hospital where you got the sharps from in the first place.
